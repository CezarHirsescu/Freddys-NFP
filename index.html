Climate and Radiant Environment

C.A.R.E is a Not for Profit organization that is dedicated to spreading awareness about the detrimental effects that climate change and rising sea levels have on the environment. Learn more on the "about" page

How Climate Change is Affecting the Pacific Ocean

In the pacific ocean, the sea levels are rising 2-3 times more than the global average

By 2100, the US national climate assessment predicts that the sea level rising scenario will range from 0.2 - 2.0 meters.

The main reason for its higher rate of increase compared to other areas is due to its close proximity with melting glaciers and the thermal expansion of seawater (salt water).

Salt water boils faster than clean/fresh water because it will be harder for the water molecules to escape, making it boil faster as it requires less energy.

Adding salt to water is going to do two things to water's physical properties: it will raise the boiling point and it will lower the specific heat. ... But lowering the water's specific heat — AKA, the amount of energy needed to change an object's temperature — will cause the salt water to heat up faster!

Real Life Examples

Island of Kiribati

Kiribati is known to be one of the first few countries to be seriously threatened by climate change and its cause of sea levels to rise. The island is above barely 2 meters above sea level and it is currently going through a crisis of flooding, water contamination, and food scarcity

Economy of Kiribati

Most of Kiribati's income comes from fishing in their biodiverse coral reefs. However, their economy is heavily influenced by foreign government aid, fishing exports, coconut exports and tourism. They have many ongoing projects that are all affected by sea levels rising in a negative manner(aka. Climate change) including: road rehabilitation, water and sanitation projects, and renovations to international airports.
